Did you know?

Literacy struggles disproportionately impact refugee communities, who face interrupted schooling, language barriers, and trauma, leading to lasting gaps in reading and comprehension.

Additionally, these effects often are often invisible, manifesting in lower functional and health literacy in displaced communities after resettlement.

Awareness is key to combat structural literacy barriers. Learn more at World Literacy Foundation.

Did you know?

Neuroscience shows that learning to read strengthens white matter pathways in the brain.

These connections allow different regions to communicate more effectively, supporting reading fluency, comprehension, memory, and empathy.

Early literacy physically shapes the brain for the better. Learn more at World Literacy Foundation.

80% of low-income children lose reading skills over summer breaks due to lack of access to books.

August 25, 2025

Did you know?

Poor health literacy

  • is a direct result of low literacy rates
  • is generational—a mother’s literacy directly impacts the health of her child
  • is dangerous—reading skills are essential to be able to understand important health information and make safe choices

Low literacy rates are also a mental health risk—anxiety and depression are more common in people with low literacy.

Illiteracy has impacts beyond academia. We must work to create a safer world. Learn more at World Literacy Foundation.
